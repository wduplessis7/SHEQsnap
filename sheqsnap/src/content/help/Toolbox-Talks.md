# Toolbox Talks

---

## What is a Toolbox Talk?

A Toolbox Talk is a short, focused safety briefing — typically 10 to 15 minutes — held at the start of a shift or before a task begins. A supervisor or safety officer leads the discussion, covering a specific safety topic that is relevant to the team's work.

SHEQSnap's AI-powered Toolbox Talk Generator creates a ready-to-use briefing based on real incidents and near misses from your site, ensuring the content is directly relevant to your team's experience.

---

## Accessing Toolbox Talks

Click **Toolbox Talks** in the sidebar.

This feature requires the **AI Intelligence** module to be enabled on your SHEQSnap license. If the menu item is not visible, contact your administrator.

---

## Generating a Toolbox Talk

1. Set the **date range** to pull in relevant incidents and near misses (default: last 30 days)
2. Review the list of records selected for inclusion — check or uncheck individual records as needed
3. Optionally enter a specific **Topic** in the topic field (e.g. "Electrical Safety", "Manual Handling", "Working at Heights") — leave blank to let the AI choose the most relevant topic based on the data
4. Click **Generate Toolbox Talk**
5. Wait up to 60 seconds for the AI to produce the briefing

---

## Toolbox Talk Output

The generated briefing includes the following sections:

| Section | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Title** | The safety topic for the briefing |
| **Date** | Date the talk was generated |
| **Facilitator** | Suggested facilitator role |
| **Duration** | Estimated running time |
| **Safety Message** | An opening statement to set the tone for the discussion |
| **Key Points** | Three focused safety areas for the team to be aware of |
| **Discussion Questions** | Questions to encourage participation and check understanding |
| **Action Items** | Specific follow-up tasks to be completed after the briefing |
| **Takeaway Message** | A closing statement to reinforce the main lesson |

---

## Printing and Using the Talk

1. Click **Print** to produce a printer-friendly version of the briefing
2. Run the talk with your team at the start of a shift or before a high-risk task
3. Record team attendance separately in your site safety register

The printed output is formatted for easy reading in field conditions.

---

## Tips for Effective Toolbox Talks

- Keep the session under 15 minutes to maintain attention
- Encourage team members to ask questions and share their own experiences
- Link the topic to a real recent incident or near miss on site — the AI does this automatically when records are included
- Follow up on action items at the next briefing to demonstrate accountability
- Rotate facilitators where possible to build safety ownership across the team
