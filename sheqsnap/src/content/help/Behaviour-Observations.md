# Behaviour Observations

---

## What is a Behaviour Observation?

A Behaviour Observation is a structured record of observed worker behaviour on site. Based on the principles of Behaviour-Based Safety (BBS), observations help identify both safe and at-risk behaviours before they lead to incidents. The goal is not to catch people doing things wrong — it is to reinforce safe practices and correct at-risk behaviours through coaching and engagement.

---

## When to Log an Observation

Log a Behaviour Observation whenever you:

- Witness a worker or team performing a task that could benefit from feedback
- Observe unsafe or at-risk behaviour that needs to be addressed
- Want to recognise and reinforce safe behaviour on site
- Conduct a formal BBS walkthrough or field inspection

Regular observations help build a positive safety culture by making safety conversations part of everyday work.

---

## Observation Types Explained

| Observation Type | Description |
|-----------------|-------------|
| **Safe Behaviours** | Actions, practices, or conditions that comply with procedures and reduce risk |
| **Unsafe / At-Risk Behaviours** | Actions or conditions that deviate from safe practices and could lead to harm |

When completing an observation, you can record any combination of safe and at-risk behaviours. Each observation can also have follow-up corrective actions assigned to ensure at-risk behaviours are addressed.

---

## Risk Category

The **Risk category** field classifies the type of hazard or safety area the observation relates to:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **PPE** | Missing or incorrect personal protective equipment |
| **Housekeeping** | Untidy work areas, blocked exits, poor waste management |
| **Equipment** | Damaged tools, improper use of machinery |
| **Procedure** | Deviating from a safe work procedure or permit |
| **Environment** | Lighting, noise, temperature, ventilation issues |
| **Ergonomics** | Incorrect lifting technique, repetitive strain risk |
| **Other** | Any hazard not covered by the above categories |

---

## How to Log an Observation

1. Click **Behaviour Obs.** in the sidebar
2. Click **+ New Observation**
3. Work through the four steps:
   - **Step 1 — Observation Details:** Date, location, observer, work type, and who was observed
   - **Step 2 — Task & Behaviour:** Describe the task and list any safe or at-risk behaviours
   - **Step 3 — Hazards, Risk & Actions:** Identify hazards, set a risk level, select a risk category, and record any immediate actions taken
   - **Step 4 — Engagement & Actions:** Record whether the worker was engaged, their feedback, and any follow-up actions to assign
4. Click **Submit Observation**

---

## Follow-up Actions

You can assign corrective actions directly from within an observation. These appear in the Actions Register and can be tracked to completion. All overdue actions linked to observations are highlighted in the summary dashboard at the top of the Observations list.

---

## Tips

- Be specific when describing behaviours — avoid vague entries like "bad behaviour"
- Always engage with the worker when safe to do so; coaching is more effective than reporting
- High-risk observations should be escalated promptly and have actions assigned before leaving the site
