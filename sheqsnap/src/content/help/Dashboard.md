# Dashboard

The Dashboard is the first page you see after logging in. It gives you an at-a-glance view of your organisation's safety performance.

---

## Dashboard Layout

The Dashboard is divided into three main sections:

1. **Filter Bar** — at the top, allows you to filter all data by date range and department
2. **KPI Cards** — a row of summary statistics
3. **Charts** — visual breakdowns of your safety data

---

## KPI Cards

Each card shows a key number that tells you the current state of your safety programme.

| Card | What It Shows |
|------|---------------|
| **Total Near Misses** | Total number of near miss reports in the selected period |
| **Total Incidents** | Total number of incident records in the selected period |
| **Open Actions** | Number of corrective actions that are still open or in progress |
| **Overdue Actions** | Number of actions where the due date has passed and they are not completed |
| **Pending Approvals** | Number of records waiting for an approver to review |
| **Log Entries** | Total number of log register entries in the selected period |

Cards with a red or amber background indicate something requires attention — for example, a high number of overdue actions.

---

## Charts

### Incidents by Severity (Bar Chart)

Shows how many incidents were logged at each severity level (Low, Medium, High, Critical) in the selected period. Use this to spot whether serious incidents are increasing over time.

### Near Misses by Department (Pie Chart)

Shows the proportion of near miss reports from each department. Departments with a larger slice are reporting more near misses — which can indicate either more hazards or a stronger safety reporting culture.

### Monthly Trend (Line Chart)

Shows the number of near misses and incidents logged each month over the past 12 months. Rising lines may indicate an increase in hazards or improved reporting. Flat or falling lines can indicate reporting fatigue.

### Actions Status (Bar Chart)

Shows how many actions are in each status (Open, In Progress, Completed, Overdue, Cancelled). A large "Overdue" bar is a warning sign that corrective actions are not being completed on time.

---

## Filters

At the top of the Dashboard you can apply two filters:

| Filter | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Date Range** | Select a start and end date to limit all cards and charts to that period. Defaults to the last 30 days. |
| **Department** | Select a specific department to see only records from that department. Leave blank to see all departments. |

Filters apply to all KPI cards and all charts simultaneously.

---

## Overdue Indicators

- Overdue items are highlighted in **red** across the system
- The **Overdue Actions** KPI card turns red when the count is greater than zero
- On the Actions page, overdue rows are highlighted with a red background
- The **Pending Approvals** card increases whenever a Contractor submits a record

If you see red indicators on the Dashboard, navigate to the relevant module to investigate and resolve them.
